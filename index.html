Our Transparent Evaluation Framework

Every tool is evaluated using the same 5 criteria. This transparency is the foundation of ClickWise's credibility.

How We Evaluate Tools

Our framework is grounded in concrete criteria that matter to student privacy, security, and academic integrity.

üìä

Data Collection

What we assess: Does it ask for unnecessary personal information? Can you use it anonymously?

Low: Minimal info. Optional fields.

Medium: Basic account. Some tracking.

High: Excessive info. Data harvesting.

üîê

Data Protection

What we assess: Is data encrypted? Security certifications? Breach history?

Low: HTTPS. Audited. No breaches.

Medium: Has HTTPS. Limited audits.

High: No HTTPS. Breach history.

üì±

Third-Party Sharing

What we assess: Do they sell data? Hidden trackers? Ad networks?

Low: No selling. No ads. Clear policy.

Medium: Unclear. Some ads. Cookies.

High: Sells data. Heavy tracking.

‚úÖ

Legal Compliance

What we assess: COPPA (under 13)? FERPA (student records)? Protection laws?

Low: COPPA & FERPA compliant.

Medium: Partially compliant.

High: No compliance.

üéì

Academic Integrity

What we assess: Designed to teach? Or enable cheating? Critical thinking?

Low: Guides learning. Anti-cheating.

Medium: Mix of both. Can enable plagiarism.

High: Facilitates cheating.

Risk Rating Scale

‚úì LOW RISK

Safe to use. Transparent practices. Designed for learning.

Examples: Khanmigo, Wolfram Alpha, Quizlet

‚ö† MEDIUM RISK

Use with caution. Review privacy settings. Be mindful.

Examples: ChatGPT, Photomath, Brainly

‚úó HIGH RISK

Avoid if possible. Privacy violations. Not recommended.

Examples: Essay mills, sketchy sites
